How this calculator works

The result compounds your current savings plus future monthly contributions at a fixed annual rate, then applies the classic 4% safe withdrawal rate (Bengen 1994) for retirement income.

Frequently asked questions

What is the 4% rule?

A rule of thumb suggesting a 4% annual withdrawal from your portfolio at retirement should historically last 30 years. Based on William Bengen's 1994 research.

What return rate should I assume?

The S&P 500 averaged ~10% annually since 1926. A conservative planning number is 6-7% to account for inflation and fees.

Does this account for inflation?

No. Results are in nominal dollars. For real (inflation-adjusted) numbers, subtract 2-3% from your return rate.

Does this include Social Security?

No. Add your expected Social Security or government pension separately for a complete picture.
